The Mavs' offseason is heating up, and fans are getting restless. With a core of Luka, Kyrie, and a bunch of promising young players, there's a sense that something's gotta give. Specifically, [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) and others are wondering if it's time to trade Daniel Gafford.

Gafford's been a disappointment this season, and his health concerns are piling up. [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) isn't mincing words: "We are paying him 17 mil to get outplayed by Dwight Powell." Ouch. The general consensus seems to be that Gafford's value is at an all-time low, making him a prime candidate for a trade.

So, who might be interested in Gafford? [RoyTarpleysGhost](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=881) suggests the Lakers, Raptors, Hawks, or Bulls. And what might the Mavs get in return? A 2026 first-round pick? [RoyTarpleysGhost](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=881) thinks that's a fair price. But [loki](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390) is skeptical: "I don't think he's ever returning more than a late FRP."

Some fans are proposing trades that make you go "huh." Like [RoyTarpleysGhost](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=881)'s suggestion of trading Gafford for Zaccharie Risacher, the Hawks' former first-overall pick who's been struggling to find his footing. Risacher's been averaging 6.8ppg on 34% FG in his last 7 games, and [RasheedsBigWhiteSpot](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=171) isn't impressed: "Risacher is, um, not good. Use this as a league-wide barometer: He was a former 1st overall pick and at the Rising Stars game he barely got off the bench." Harsh, but fair.

On the other hand, some fans are excited about the possibility of acquiring Jaden Ivey or RJ Barrett. But [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) is lukewarm on Ivey: "I have less hope for Ivey. He is 24 and in his fourth year in the league. I realize some of it is probably injury related, but he is not a guy I would gamble on."

The Mavs' front office has some tough decisions to make this offseason. Will they trade Gafford and try to recoup some value, or will they give him another chance to prove himself? And what about PJ Washington, who's been holding people back, according to [RoyTarpleysGhost](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=881)? One thing's for sure: Mavs fans are ready for some action.

As for this writer, I'm calling it now: the Mavs will trade Gafford for a lightly-used asset and a draft pick. And we'll all be like, "meh, okay." But hey, at least it'll be interesting to see what the front office comes up with. And who knows, maybe they'll surprise us with a splashy move. A fan can dream, right?
