## The Wolves Are Packing a Punch

The Minnesota Timberwolves just took down the Denver Nuggets 113-96, and Nikola Jokic had one of the worst games of his life: 7-26 from the field, 2-10 from 3. 
> "Joker harassed into a bad shooting night. He's still a factor on defense, and he may still be miffed he finished 4th behind Wemby, Chet, and Ausar Thompson." - [HoosierDaddyKid](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159)

The T-Wolves are looking fierce, and their defense is a big reason why. Rudy Gobert is still a defensive force to be reckoned with. Ayo Dosunmu chipped in 25 points off the bench, which is a great sign for the Wolves.

## The Hawks Are Making a Statement

The Atlanta Hawks took down the Knicks 109-108, with CJ McCollum hitting a game-winner for the second straight game. 
> "Hawks had an excellent trade deadline. I expect the Knicks to win a tough series still, but the Hawks are definitely stating their case." - [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404)

The Hawks are looking like a team on the rise, with young talent like Jonathan Kuminga making big contributions off the bench. Meanwhile, the Knicks are struggling to find consistency.

## Coaching Carousel

The Chicago Bulls are searching for a new head of basketball operations, and it sounds like they have a few candidates lined up. 
> "The Chicago Bulls have started the search process for their new head of basketball operations, receiving permission to interview Minnesota's Matt Lloyd, Detroit's Dennis Lindsey, Atlanta's Bryson Graham, Cleveland's Mike Gansey and San Antonio's Dave Telep, per ESPN sources." - [Unknown](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=)

And in a related note, Billy Donovan is exiting as head coach of the Bulls after six seasons. 
> "BREAKING: Billy Donovan is exiting as head coach of the Chicago Bulls after six seasons, sources tell ESPN." - [Unknown](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=)

## Mavs Fans React

Some Mavs fans are still reeling from the team's own front office drama. 
> "I don’t trust O’Conner as far as I can throw him. Unless GS gets lucky in the lottery that team is no longer attractive at the moment. Tearing down a dynasty is going to be a long, hard process." - [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404)

Meanwhile, others are enjoying the schadenfreude of watching other teams struggle. 
> "The Bulls and Kings are just awful. Although they haven’t done as much damage as our team has done with the fanbase. We were just luckily enough to hit the lottery in one of those special years." - [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404)

## A Look Around the League

The Portland Trailblazers are in an interesting spot, with some good young talent and decent draft capital. 
> "They could be stuck as a good but not great team in the stacked west. Now they have a new owner. He has been successful in the NHL but he looks to run a real tight business without spending money." - [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404)

And in other news, Tim Hardaway Jr. is making a case for Sixth Man of the Year. 
> "Good story on potential 6MOY, Tim Hardaway Jr." - [HoosierDaddyKid](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159)

As the playoffs heat up, one thing's for sure: it's going to be a wild ride. Who will come out on top? Only time will tell. But one thing's for sure - the MavsBoard community will be keeping it lively.
