The MavsBoard community was abuzz this week with news that the Dallas Morning News had mentioned Masai Presti, the current Raptors president of basketball operations, as a potential candidate for the Mavs' top front office role. [F Gump](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412) was quick to point out that he's been touting Presti as a dream hire for months:

> For those who have wanted to laugh at my idea of Presti as the new guy in charge, the DMN is jumping on my bandwagon. They didn't mention Presti alone, but he was the 2nd name on their short list. And their article takes the very same approach I believe in - ending with the same type of people in mind - that the Mavs need an EXPERT, they have an owner with incredibly deep pockets, and he needs to hire the best. PERIOD.

[Cow](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=92) was quick to give [F Gump](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412) a hard time, jokingly correcting him: "Don't you mean, 'our idea'?"

Meanwhile, [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404) had a more nuanced take on the situation:

> Man, Dallas will need to move fast when the season ends. I just get the feeling that Ricardi and Finley will stay in similar roles, and the new guy brought in is the President of operations role and not a true GM. Ricardi and Finley will report to him. Some teams have set up like this. That person will have a lot of leadway with Dumont not interfering, but also not be the person handling the day to day scouting.

[DanSchwartzgan](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=72) agreed that this was likely the case: 

> This is probably the most likely outcome. Dumont has been willing to spend on upper tier leadership. But, you never want to tie the hands of the top guy and say you have to retain these guys that are already here. That's a surefire way to run off the very best out there who will have their own relationships they prefer.

The NBA world was also abuzz with some major milestones and achievements. [HoosierDaddyKid](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159) shared some exciting news:

> LBJ becomes the first non-point guard to reach 12,000 assists.
> Jaylen Brown is on track to finish 5th in MVP voting.
> The first game in NBA history that 3 different players had a triple-double in the same game.

And in other Mavs news, [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404) noted that Max Kleber is on track to finish the season with a 40% three-point shooting percentage:

> Per Locked On Mavs, Max averages 7 threes attempts per game. If he does that the next two games, he needs to make at least 6 out of 14 to finish the year over 40% from three. He is currently exactly at 40% for the season.

As for recent games, the Mavs fell to the Bucks 122-85 on Wednesday. On Tuesday, they lost to the Warriors 106-98. 

The Mavs' tank watch continued, with the team currently sitting at 24-52. But [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) remained optimistic:

> Issac still thinks there is a chance. He called the AD salary dump.

The Mavs' website also got a makeover, with a shiny new header that's got everyone talking. And with free agency just around the corner, fans are eagerly anticipating what moves the team will make.

The Mavs take on the Orlando Magic tonight at 7:30 PM CST. Will they be able to pull off the win? 

One thing's for sure: Mavs fans will be watching closely. And with rumors swirling around the team's next GM, it's an exciting time to be a Mavs fan. 

Prediction: Presti to Dallas by the end of the month?
