## The Smoking Cuban  

1. **[Mavericks' free‑agency gamble looks genius now as polarizing former guard hits bottom](https://thesmokingcuban.com/mavericks-free-agency-gamble-looks-genius-now-as-polarizing-former-guard-hits-bottom?utm_source=RSS)** – The Cuban argues that letting Spencer Dinwiddie slip into Europe was a masterstroke; Dallas saved cap space and avoided a likely bench‑dragging contract, while Dinwiddie’s EuroLeague stint confirms the move was financially prudent despite the fan backlash.  

2. **[Mavericks need Kyrie Irving to do something he's never done in Dallas](https://thesmokingcuban.com/mavericks-need-kyrie-irving-to-do-something-he-s-never-done-in-dallas?utm_source=RSS)** – With Luka Doncic shouldering the scoring load, the article urges Irving to become the primary playmaker, running the offense and creating for teammates—a shift that could unlock the team’s offensive ceiling and make the “Kyrie‑Luka” combo click.  

3. **[Mavericks must avoid this costly free‑agency trap they're already eyeing](https://thesmokingcuban.com/mavericks-must-avoid-this-costly-free-agency-trap-theyre-already-eyeing?utm_source=RSS)** – The piece warns against overpaying for Khris Middleton, suggesting Dallas should treat him like a veteran minimum to avoid a salary‑cap nightmare while still retaining his versatile wing production.  

4. **[Mavericks accidentally fixed a decade‑long problem in a chaos‑filled season](https://thesmokingcuban.com/mavericks-accidentally-fixed-decade-long-problem-in-chaos-filled-season?utm_source=RSS)** – A deep dive into how the 2025‑26 squad finally embraced a fast‑paced transition offense, shedding the “slow‑ball” stigma and revealing a new identity that could serve as a blueprint for the post‑Luka rebuild.  

5. **[Mavericks are officially chasing a heist the NBA will hate](https://thesmokingcuban.com/mavericks-are-officially-chasing-heist-the-nba-will-hate?utm_source=RSS)** – With the franchise openly tanking, the author details the “lottery‑maximizing” maneuvers—stocking the bench with young talent, resting starters, and even considering a mid‑season trade dump—to improve odds of landing a top‑five pick.
