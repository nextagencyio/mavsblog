The Dallas Mavericks have been in rebuild mode for most of the season, and it's been a wild ride for fans. This week, they played a trio of games against the Milwaukee Bucks, Golden State Warriors, and Cleveland Cavaliers. While the wins haven't been plentiful, there have been some encouraging signs, particularly from rookie Cooper Flagg. Let's dive into the MavsBoard community's thoughts on the games and what they've learned.

### Game 76: Dallas Mavericks (24-51) @ Milwaukee Bucks (29-45)

The Mavericks traveled to Milwaukee for a matchup against the Bucks, and fans were eager to see if they could pull off a win. Unfortunately, it wasn't meant to be. [cow](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=92) joked that "The tank tolls for thee," highlighting the team's focus on losing to secure a better draft pick. Despite being on the second night of a back-to-back, [Scott41theMavs](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=97) had faith in the team to get the job done, but it didn't happen.

### Game 72: Dallas Mavericks (23-48) vs GS Warriors (33-38)

In a game against the Golden State Warriors, Cooper Flagg had a strong performance, but it was marred by turnovers. [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404) noted that Flagg and teammate Naji Marshall combined for 15 turnovers, which was a significant factor in the team's loss. However, [cow](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=92) was optimistic about Flagg's growth, saying he played well until the Warriors started blitzing him late in the game. "He needs to work on his handle in the summer if he's going to handle the ball so much on this level," [cow](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=92) observed.

### Game 67: Dallas Mavericks (22-44) vs Cleveland Cavs (40-26)
