The Dallas Mavericks are sitting pretty with around $38 million below the luxury tax, giving them a significant amount of flexibility heading into the 2026 offseason. As our community at MavsBoard has been discussing, this opens up a world of possibilities for the front office to make some big moves.

One of the main topics of conversation has been the team's center rotation and the potential trade value of Daniel Gafford. **DanSchwartzgan** pointed out that Gafford's contract was designed to be tradeable, and with a team like the Atlanta Hawks potentially looking to make some moves, it's possible that Gafford could be on the move.

However, as **F Gump** noted, trading Gafford would require the Mavs to have a starter-caliber center in return, which might not be easy to find. "I am very hesitant to trade Gaff and end up without a starter-caliber C," he said. This is especially true considering the uncertainty surrounding Christian Wood's (or Lively's) availability for the upcoming season.

The community has also been discussing the potential for the Mavs to target a 3-and-D player in free agency. **mvossman** suggested that the team could look to overpay a little bit to get a high-level 3-and-D player, citing Corey Kispert as a potential target. However, **loki** noted that Kispert might not be the best fit, and that the team could look to pursue someone like Peyton Watson in a sign-and-trade deal.

Watson, a restricted free agent, is expected to draw interest from several teams around the league. **loki** believes that the Mavs might need to use their Trade Player Exception (TPE) to get him, which could be a max of 4 years/$90.5 million. However, **michaeltex** noted that it's unclear if the MLE (Mid-Level Exception) will be enough to land Watson, and that the Denver Nuggets might be able to match any offer.

Another potential target mentioned was Lu Dort, who is set to become a free agent. **DallasMaverick** wondered what other teams might offer for his services, and **michaeltex** floated the idea of taking on Dort's contract and getting a future protected pick in return.

The community has also been discussing the potential for the Mavs to make some moves in the draft. **MaxiThreeba** joked that he was able to get the Hawks pick into the top 3 using tankathon, but it's clear that the team could be looking to make some moves on draft night.

As the 2026 offseason approaches, it's clear that the Mavs have a lot of options on the table. Whether they choose to trade for a big man, pursue a 3-and-D player in free agency, or make some moves on draft night, MavsBoard will be here to discuss it all.
