The Dallas Mavericks' rebuild year has been a rollercoaster ride, and the recent game against the Sacramento Kings was no exception. As our community at MavsBoard discussed the game, it was clear that fans are eagerly watching the team's progress, particularly when it comes to player development. Let's dive into the conversation and see what stood out.

**Cooper Flagg's Absence and the Impact on the Team**

The game was notable for the absence of Cooper Flagg, a highly touted prospect who has been sidelined with an injury. As **Chicagojk** noted, "That was a rough watch. I skipped the last 8 minutes as my recording stopped. Not a lot to like. Defense was pretty poor." The Mavs' defense struggled, and it's clear that Flagg's presence is missed.

**Max's Struggles and the Search for a Role**

One of the main topics of discussion was Max's performance. **Chicagojk** expressed disappointment, saying, "Poor game by max on offense and defense." **FireNicoHarrison** chimed in, suggesting that Max might not be a starter on a championship team, comparing him to someone like Stevenson. On the other hand, **mvossman** offered a more nuanced view, noting that Max is a complimentary player who thrives when surrounded by talented teammates.

**AJ Johnson's Emergence and Potential**

Another player who drew attention was AJ Johnson. **DanSchwartzgan** was excited to see AJ in action, writing, "I think he can be an instant O guy off the bench." He praised AJ's offense but expressed concerns about his defense, describing him as a "total matador." Despite this, **loki** noted that AJ had a solid game and that Kidd seemed to trust him down the stretch.

**Tyus Jones' Limited Impact and Cisse's Development**

The conversation also touched on Tyus Jones, who has yet to make a significant impact. **Chicagojk** observed, "Tyus Jones really has not shown much at all." In contrast, **Chicagojk** was cautiously optimistic about Cisse, suggesting that he might benefit from another two-way contract and more development time.

**The Challenges of Player Development and the G-League**

The discussion also highlighted the challenges of player development, particularly for those who skip traditional college basketball or G-League routes. **Chicagojk** noted that players like Nemby, who have four years of competitive college experience, may have an advantage. He wondered aloud whether the G-League provides the same level of stress and competition as college basketball.

**The Coaching Staff and Front Office**

Some fans also expressed frustration with the coaching staff and front office. **loki** joked that he hopes the next GM fires Jason Kidd "into the sun." **Scott41theMavs** made a more serious point, suggesting that the transition timeline will depend on the quality of the new GM.

**Christie's Struggles and the Need for a Bucket Getter**

Finally, **Chicagojk** expressed disappointment with Christie's performance, saying, "Christie was awful again. He really needs to play with a bucket getter and point guard." This sentiment was echoed by others, who emphasized the importance of surrounding young players with talent and experience.
