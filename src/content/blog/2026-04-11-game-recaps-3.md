The Dallas Mavericks have been on a bit of a rollercoaster lately, with their recent games against the Orlando Magic and Boston Celtics showcasing both the highs and lows of their young roster. As a team in rebuild mode, the Mavs are focusing on player development, and fans are eager to see their young players grow and improve.

In the game against the Orlando Magic, the Mavs let a winnable game slip away, prompting **HoosierDaddyKid** to lament, "Mavs let Orlando off the hook, in the nick of time. This was a game they should have won." The team's struggles with consistency were evident, and fans were left wondering what could have been.

One bright spot, however, has been the play of Klay Thompson. **RBB** noted that Klay is having a nice game, and it's always good to see. As the trade deadline approaches, fans are wondering where Klay will be playing next season. Will he be a part of the Mavs' long-term plans, or will he be dealt to a contending team?

The Mavs' next game was against the Boston Celtics, where they faced a tough test in Jayson Tatum's season debut. **cow** was excited to see Tatum back on the court, noting that his return will be interesting to watch, especially in terms of his injury recovery. Tatum's performance was a mixed bag, with some rust evident in his play. **Scott41theMavs** joked that Tatum and Cooper Flagg were having a "miss-a-thon" between them, highlighting the struggles of both players.

Speaking of Cooper Flagg, the rookie has been facing some growing pains lately. **GATA** noted that Flagg is struggling, and that his injury really derailed his game. **ThisIStheYear** was blunt, saying that the ROY race is settled, implying that Flagg's chances of winning the award are slim. However, it's essential to remember that Flagg is still a young player learning the ropes, and his development is crucial to the Mavs' long-term success.

The Mavs' offense has been a concern lately, with **Chicagojk** expressing frustration with the team's lack of creativity. "I really don’t like the offense of just having [Cooper Flagg] try to drive every possession with everyone standing," he wrote. "Maybe at times but throw another guard out there. It is boring to watch too." **cow** echoed these sentiments, saying that the team's offense is too reliant on Klay pulling the trigger from beyond the arc.

Despite these struggles, there are still plenty of reasons to be excited about the Mavs' young core. **Mavs2021** praised Dwight Powell's play, noting that he was a key factor in the team's efforts. **Scott41theMavs** also highlighted the strong play of Naji Martin and Moussa Cissé, who have been providing a spark off the bench.

As the Mavs continue to rebuild, fans are eager to see their young players develop and grow. The team's focus on player development is evident, and it's exciting to think about what the future holds. As **michaeltex** noted, it's frustrating to see so many players on the injury report, but it's all part of the process.
