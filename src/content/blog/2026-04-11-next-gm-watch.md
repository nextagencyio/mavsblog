The Dallas Mavericks' front office shakeup has been a hot topic among fans, and the MavsBoard community has been buzzing with ideas on who should take the reins as the team's next General Manager or President of Basketball Operations. As we approach the end of the season, the speculation is heating up, and our community members are weighing in with their thoughts on the ideal candidate.

For those who may not be familiar, the Mavericks recently parted ways with their current GM, Nico Harrison, in a move that surprised some but was seen as a necessary step by many fans. With owner Mark Cuban willing to spend top dollar to bring in the best talent, the question on everyone's mind is: who should be the next leader of the Mavs' front office?

**The Case for Outside Expertise**

Many fans, like **F Gump**, believe that the Mavericks need to bring in an outside expert with a proven track record of success. "I think the Mavs need an EXPERT, they have an owner with incredibly deep pockets, and he needs to hire the best. PERIOD," F Gump wrote. This sentiment was echoed by **loki**, who suggested Zach Kleiman, the Memphis Grizzlies' GM, as a potential candidate. "He drafts well, gets good value in trades, and is theoretically more obtainable than Presti," loki noted.

**The Presti Pitch**

Speaking of Billy Presti, the Chicago Bulls' GM, he's been a popular name among Mavs fans. **F Gump** initially floated Presti's name as a potential candidate, and the Dallas Morning News recently mentioned him as a possibility. Presti's reputation as a savvy and aggressive front office executive has fans excited about the prospect of him leading the Mavs.

**The Kidd Conundrum**

However, not everyone is convinced that bringing in an outside expert is the way to go. Some fans are concerned that Jason Kidd, the current Mavs coach, might be promoted to a front office role. **Chicagojk** expressed skepticism about Kidd's qualifications, saying, "I would not be in favor of Kidd being GM. When Ricardi named popped up someone said if he got the job that it would mean Kidd has influence." **cow** was more blunt, stating, "Kidd would be disaster."

**Front Office Structure**

The discussion also turned to the potential structure of the front office. **Chicagojk** suggested that the Mavs might adopt a model where the President of Basketball Operations has significant influence, but the day-to-day scouting and GM duties are handled by someone else. **DanSchwartzgan** cautioned against tying the hands of the new front office executive, saying, "You never want to tie the hands of the top guy and say you have to retain these guys that are already here."

**Other Candidates**

As the conversation continued, other candidates emerged. **Unknown** suggested Bob Myers, the former Golden State Warriors GM, as a potential fit. **ThisIStheYear** gave credit to the current front office for their handling of the Anthony Davis trade, saying, "The current team did a pretty good job with the Davis trade. Credit where credit is due."
