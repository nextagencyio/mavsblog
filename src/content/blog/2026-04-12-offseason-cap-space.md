The Dallas Mavericks are currently sitting pretty with approximately $38 million below the luxury tax threshold, giving them ample room to maneuver in the 2026 offseason. As the team looks to build on their recent successes, MavsBoard fans are diving deep into the salary cap situation, proposing trades, and identifying potential free agency targets. Let's dive into some of the most insightful discussions.

### Center Rotation Conundrum

One of the biggest challenges facing the Mavs is figuring out their center rotation. With [Dereck Lively II](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=72) coming off a significant injury, fans are debating the need for a reliable backup center. [DanSchwartzgan](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=72) suggests that the team needs an entire center rotation solution, potentially including a functional third-stringer and a two-way spot dedicated to the position.

### Trade Talks: Gafford and Beyond

The Mavs have a valuable trade asset in [Daniel Gafford](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412), whose contract was designed to be tradeable. [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) believes that Gafford is the kind of asset you take a shot with, but acknowledges that it's hard to get it right. One potential trade proposal involves sending Gafford to the Atlanta Hawks for [Corey Kispert](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390), a sharpshooter who could help the Mavs' offense.

### Free Agency Targets

In free agency, the Mavs are expected to have their sights set on players who can fill specific needs. [Chicagojk](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=404) suggests that instead of signing an established player to the Mid-Level Exception (MLE), the team could look for a diamond in the rough who hasn't hit yet. One potential target is [Peyton Watson](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390), a 3-and-D player who could be a great fit alongside Luka Doncic.

### Luxury Tax and Apron Considerations

As the Mavs navigate the salary cap, they need to be mindful of the luxury tax and apron implications. [DanSchwartzgan](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=72) notes that being under the tax doesn't matter, as the first apron will be the limiting factor for the team. This means that the Mavs will need to be strategic in their spending to avoid being hard-capped.

### Other Targets and Proposals

Other potential targets and proposals discussed on MavsBoard include:

* [Jordan Ivey](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412), a young guard who could be a reclamation project
* [Haywood Highsmith](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159), a 3-and-D player who recently signed with the Phoenix Suns
* [Luca Vildoza](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=23), a European prospect who could be a draft target

### Conclusion

As the 2026 offseason approaches, MavsBoard fans are engaged in lively discussions about the team's salary cap situation, potential trades, and free agency targets. With a deep understanding of the team's needs and the salary cap implications, fans are proposing creative solutions to help the Mavs build a championship roster. By listening to the insights and opinions of the MavsBoard community, we can gain a better understanding of what the future holds for the Dallas Mavericks.
