The Dallas Mavericks are sitting pretty with around $38M below the luxury tax, and MavsBoard fans are going wild with proposals on how to spend it. The team's situation with center Dereck Lively, who's coming off an injury, has fans thinking they need a reliable center rotation solution. 

Some fans, like [DanSchwartzgan](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=72), believe that with Lively on the roster, the team needs an entire center rotation solution for the games he'll miss. This could mean a functional third-stringer and potentially a two-way spot dedicated to the position. 

Others think it's time to trade for or sign a 3&D wing. [mvossman](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=482) suggests targeting a player like Moody, who can guard up and has a 7'1" wingspan. He's a better defender than Maxi Kleber and has similar offensive capabilities. 

The MLE (Mid-Level Exception) is a hot topic, with some fans suggesting the team use it to sign a reclamation project like Ivey. However, [F Gump](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412) isn't having it: "Ivey? If he's more than MLE, for me it's an instant hard pass. And the closer I look, I am not sure he's the guy you want anyhow, even at MLE or less." 

[F Gump](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=412) also thinks that trading Daniel Gafford, who's set to get a raise, might not be the best idea, especially with Lively's injury history. "You can't assume Lively is the answer as the C you need, and move Gafford. That's a major mistake." 

One proposal gaining traction is trading Gafford to the Atlanta Hawks for Corey Kispert, a sharpshooter on a sub-MLE contract. [loki](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390) thinks this could be a good move: "I'd rather have Kispert than pay Gafford $54M over the next 3 seasons." 

The team's salary situation also has fans considering sign-and-trade deals. [loki](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390) thinks Peyton Watson, a 3&D wing, could be a great target: "My primary target in free agency would be Peyton Watson (S&T). It's unlikely they can pull the deal off, but I think he could fill that 3&D role long-term." 

As for other teams' players, [DallasMaverick](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=352) wonders what it would take to pry away Oklahoma City's Luguentz Dort: "Can we provide a competitive offer to OKC? And what will Dort want in a new contract?" 

The Mavs' cap space and flexibility have fans dreaming big. With the right moves, this team could get a lot better – or a lot worse. One thing's for sure: MavsBoard fans will be keeping a close eye on the front office's moves. 

As [MaxiThreeba](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=333) ominously warns, "It only took me 4 tries on tankathon to get the Hawks pick (Spurs have swap rights) into the top 3. Based on SAS lottery luck, I half expect that to happen. And the thought of one of the top 3-4 dudes in their lap is even more terrifying." 

Time will tell if the Mavs make any big moves this offseason. Until then, MavsBoard fans will keep on speculating – and loving every minute of it. 

In the end, if I had to put my money on it, I'd say the Mavs splurge on a 3&D wing and overpay a little for someone like Watson or Moody. A center rotation solution can wait; a wing who can guard and shoot is a lot harder to find. Mark my words: come next season, the Mavs will be rocking a revamped roster with a shiny new 3&D wing.
