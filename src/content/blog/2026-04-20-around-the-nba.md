## The Unstoppable Pistons?
The Detroit Pistons are leading the East from wire to wire, and [HoosierDaddyKid](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159) thinks they're being disrespected: 

> "I find it very odd when talking about what team will come out the East, nobody thinks Detroit will. It's either Boston, NY, or Cleveland. Detroit has led the East from wire to wire, suffered through suspensions and Cade's injury and still was 1st across the finish line."

Their strong defense and inside play have earned them the top spot, and [Mavs2021](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=504) thinks Cade should take it personally and prove the doubters wrong. Can they keep their momentum going?

## Playoff Upsets and Surprises
The Orlando Magic's dominating win over the Charlotte Hornets caught many off guard, including [Scott41theMavs](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=97): 

> "Orlando beating Charlotte is not a huge surprise. Orlando beating the poop out of Charlotte is. I'm sure that LaMelo finding out that people abhor basketball players who act like it's the WWE and they're heels played a part in his rather woeful game."

Meanwhile, [cow](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=92) was shocked by Andre Drummond's dagger three. These playoffs have already brought many unexpected moments!

## Team Building Challenges
The Mavs' own team building road ahead looks tough, according to [Scott41theMavs](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=97): 

> "The Mavs have an extremely difficult team building road ahead of them with no pick of their own '27-'30. But at least they have the bedrock to work with."

The team's future, and Kyrie's, are always a topic of discussion. Will he ask for a trade, and where might he go? [loki](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=390) thinks disgruntled Kyrie has negative trade value.

## Western Woes and Mavs Musings
In the West, [Dahlsim](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=9) notes that the Warriors got some real help from ex-Mavs vet Kristaps Porzingis. The Blazers' fate might also impact the Mavs' future picks. [RasheedsBigWhiteSpot](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=171) thinks we should root for the Blazers to get a higher Suns' 2nd rounder.

## A Take on Kon and Flagg
[FireNicoHarrison](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=4633) called Kon a bluff, saying he's not close to Flagg. [RasheedsBigWhiteSpot](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=171) corrected that narrative, saying Kon was only the 5th factor in Charlotte's turnaround: 

> "CORRECTION: 2 minutes of regulation AND ALL OF OT. Acting like Kon was anything other than maaaaaaaybe the 5th factor in Charlotte's turn around is a narrative basketball myth in the making."

As the playoffs continue, one thing is for sure: Mavs fans have plenty to say about it all! 

## A Prediction to End On
As we wrap up this roundup, a bold prediction from [HoosierDaddyKid](https://www.mavsboard.com/member.php?action=profile&uid=159): "Orlando ain't sh!t  They need to blow that joint to smithereens!" Who will come out on top in the end? Only time will tell!
